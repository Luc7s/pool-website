Our Pool Materials

We will aid you to find the look, feel, and durability that works for you.

Finishes: The materials that coat the inside of your pool.

  • Pebble Finish

    Pebble finishes are low maintenance, anti-slip, and non-porous material, with various color options.

    A pebble finish surface is a mixture of hydraulic cement and polished stone pebbles.

  • White Plaster (Marcite)

    White plaster finishes are a popular choice for being a smooth, bright, and low-cost pool finish option.

    A white plaster finish typically lasts five to seven years before any signs of etching or chipping appear.

  • Diamond Brite®

    Diamond Brite finishes are known to give pools a luxurious and high-end look and comfortable to swimmers' feet.

    Diamond Brite is an exposed aggregate pool finish blended from natural quartz aggregate, polymer-modified cement, and non-fading color quartz in a stunning variety of colors.

  • QuartzScapes®

    QuartzScapes® finishes are considered the best of the quartz pool finishes, often spoken about as the best of the pool finishes.

    A quartz finish is a blend of crushed quartz, white cement, and mineral-based pigments for its color.

    A quartz pool finish typically lasts between seven and twelve years.

This is just a small selection of the pool finishes we can provide for you!

Our Deck Materials

We will aid you to find the look, feel, and durability that works for you.

Pavers: The material that surrounds the outside of your pool.

    • Granite Pavers: Granite Paving offers a luxury finish to both contemporary and traditional settings.
    • Bluestone Pavers: Bluestone is made of quartz particles and sand compounds and is usually cut into rectangles or squares. Bluestone comes in a range of colors, blue to purple to green and everything between.
    • Brick Pavers: Brick pavers are durable and give off a classical feel to whatever area they get placed in. If you are looking for something natural looking brick pavers, offer various earthy-style color options.
    • Travertine Pavers: Travertine pavers are a great pool deck option for hot climates as they stay cooler than other types of natural or manufactured stone. Travertine pavers receive praise for giving off an Old World, traditional or rustic look.
    • Concrete Pavers: Concrete pavers will typically get chosen as a cheaper alternative to brick and clay pavers. Concrete pavers have a wide selection of color options but do not have natural pigmentation, so they tend to fade.
    • Limestone Pavers: Limestone pavers, similar to Travertine pavers, are of natural stone. Limestone pavers, you will commonly see in small format, plank, and slab designs.
    • Sandstone Pavers: Sandstone pavers is naturally non-slip, making them an excellent option for pool decks.
    • Clay Pavers: Clay brick pavers give the best of both worlds, offering a beautiful aged look while being less averse to cracks and chipping.
